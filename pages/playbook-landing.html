AI Playbook Landing Page

Like any technology, AI can be helpful, harmful, or ineffectual. The CMS AI Playbook shares best practices for applying AI to ensure that CMS stakeholder needs are met. The HHS Trustworthy AI Playbook explains how agencies across the department can derive maximum value from AI while mitigating risks.

At CMS, AI has the power to reshape the way we use data to make decisions. In fact, there is no better place to implement AI technology. AI is driven by data, and CMS is an extremely data-rich agency. There are nearly 150 million Americans enrolled in Medicare, Medicaid, and the Child Health Insurance Program. We house patient and provider claims, enrollments, and medical records, along with internal data such as budget documents and contractor data.

AI can find patterns in this data that may not be accessible via manual methods of analysis, either because it would take too long or because a human can only process so much information at once. Aside from sorting the data, AI can help us to learn from it. Further, AI can automate labor-intensive manual processes, find operational efficiencies, provide insights from information, make predictions, identify disparities, and reveal new ways of solving problems.

In short, AI is an emerging technology that should have a wide impact throughout CMS and ultimately, for the American public. These impacts include resource savings and better health outcomes.

To implement AI, and to do so responsibly, we must educate our workforce, share knowledge, be transparent about our use of data, follow ethical standards, and experiment with new technologies.

That is why we have created this website. It offers a starting point for anyone interested in any aspect of AI at CMS.
