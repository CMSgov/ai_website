HHS Trustworthy AI Playbook

There are several ethical considerations to address when designing AI-enabled systems. These considerations are explained in the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services’ Trustworthy AI Playbook, which was released in September 2021. The Handbook covers six overarching principles of Responsible AI, based on Executive Order 13960, “Promoting the Use of Trustworthy Artificial Intelligence in the Federal Government, and OMB Memorandum M-21-06, “Guidance for Regulation of Artificial Intelligence Applications.”

The playbook demands that AI adhere to six principles. It should be fair and impartial; transparent and explainable; responsible and accountable; robust and reliable; private; safe and secure. Additionally, the playbook lists concrete steps we can take to encode these principles while designing AI solutions.

Some examples are:

  • Obtaining consent from patients for the use of mobile data that will be used in an app to track disease symptoms (Privacy)
  • Including data encryption, user authentication, and other applicable security controls in a NLP-based AI application that interprets handwritten medical records (Safe/Secure)
  • Establishing clear roles and responsibilities for people involved in creating AI solutions, especially so that specific people are accountable when solutions do not perform adequately (Responsible/Accountable)

The six principles of Responsible AI must be tended to throughout the data life cycle, from initial conception of a solution to its operation and maintenance. For more detailed information, refer to the Trustworthy AI Playbook.
