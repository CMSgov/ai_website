Artificial Intelligence at CMS

At CMS, AI has the power to reshape the way we use data to make decisions. In fact, there is no better place to implement AI technology. AI is driven by data, and CMS is an extremely data-rich agency.

To implement AI, and to do so responsibly, we must educate our workforce, share knowledge with our partners, follow ethical standards, and experiment with new methodologies.

That is why we have created this website. It offers a starting point for anyone interested in any aspect of AI at CMS.

Leveraging the power of AI to serve America's healthcare needs

There are nearly 150 million Americans enrolled in Medicare, Medicaid, and the Child Health Insurance Program. We house patient and provider claims, enrollments, and medical records, along with internal data such as budget documents and contractor data.

AI can find patterns in this data that may not be accessible via manual methods of analysis, either because it would take too long or because a human can only process so much information at once. Aside from sorting the data, AI can help us to learn from it. Further, AI can automate labor-intensive manual processes, find operational efficiencies, provide insights from information, make predictions, identify disparities, and reveal new ways of solving problems.

In short, AI is an emerging technology that should have a wide impact throughout CMS and ultimately, for the American public. These impacts include resource savings and better health outcomes.

CMS AI Resources

Those who wish to engage in AI-related activities, as either a CMS employee, partner, or vendor, should be aware of federal policies regarding the application of AI. Following are links to foundational governance documents on AI and other related resources:

  • Executive Order 13859: Maintaining American Leadership in Artificial Intelligence

    This 2019 Executive Order sets forth a federal strategy for promoting advancements in AI technology and innovation while protecting American technology, economic and national security, civil liberties, privacy, and American values and enhancing international and industry collaboration with foreign partners and allies.

  • National Artificial Intelligence Act of 2020

    The National AI Initiative Act of 2020 (DIVISION E, SEC. 5001) became law on January 1, 2021, providing for a coordinated program across the entire Federal government to accelerate AI research and application for the Nation’s economic prosperity and national security. The mission of the National AI Initiative is to ensure continued U.S.leadership in AI research and development, lead the world in the development and use of trustworthy AI in the public and private sectors, and prepare the present and future U.S. workforce for the integration of AI systems across all sectors of the economy and society.

  • The National Artificial Intelligence Initiative

    This website contains news and information about the five pillars of the national AI strategy as outlined in the National AI Initiative Act of 2020. Those pillars are: innovation, advancing trustworthy AI, education and training, infrastructure, applications, and international cooperation.

  • CMS AI Playbook

    Does AI sound as overwhelming as it does promising? Don’t worry. The CMS AI Playbook is an easy-to-follow guide that walks stakeholders through the process of conceiving, developing, and implementing AI solutions at CMS. It covers design principles, research and development, engineering, and procurement.

    The guidelines in the AI Playbook cover the technical aspects of development as well as human factors. They are not just for software engineers. They are invaluable to anybody at CMS who believes an AI solution can improve their team or provide value to their customer. Successful application of AI must take into account the customer perspective; it requires well-tailored, high quality data sources; it must be secure; and last but not least, it must follow rigorous ethical standards.

    To learn more about how to responsibly, effectively, and efficiently devise AI solutions, check out the official CMS AI Playbook

  • HHS Trustworthy AI Playbook

    The goal of Trustworthy AI is to maximize the benefits while minimizing the risks of novel AI-enabled technologies. The Playbook covers six overarching principles of Responsible AI, based on Executive Order 13960, “Promoting the Use of Trustworthy Artificial Intelligence in the Federal Government," and OMB Memorandum M-21-06, “Guidance for Regulation of Artificial Intelligence Applications.” According to the Playbook, AI should be: fair and impartial; transparent and explainable; responsible and accountable; robust and reliable; private; safe and secure. Additionally, the playbook lists concrete steps we can take to encode these principles while designing AI solutions.

Programs and Initatives

AI will play a major role in the future of CMS, but it is also already here. Below you can find details on AI programs and initatives that are revolutionizing CMS operations. Keep reading to learn more about how CMS is applying AI techniques to increase efficiency, improve health outcomes, and detect waste, abuse, and fraud.

  • CMS AI Explorers Program

    The AI Explorers Program is an exciting opportunity for CMS employees to prototype AI applications for testing and potential development. For two years, OIT will accept proposals for projects that apply AI and ML techniques related to CMS's mission and to the applicant's team functional area. From these submissions, OIT executive leadership will support up to two projects per quarter. Support includes funding and matching with pre-qualified vendors. Awardees will have three to six months to test their hypotheses.

    The goals of the pilot are to:

    • bootstrap both proofs of value and initial Minimum Viable Products
    • encourage applicants to get acquainted with AI/ML and grow their technological skill sets
    • test out hypotheses using Agile methodologies prior to a large-scale development effort
    • develop incremental expertise, and boost confidence in the AI domain
    • establish a baseline framework on how to bring an AI/ML project from ideation to production readiness within CMS

  • Workforce Resilience Program

    Workforce Resilience (WR) is the CMS program designed to cultivate a resilient and sustainable workforce for the future. The intent is to both instill a culture of learning and create a space for everyone to innovate and grow professionally. This way, we can provide even more value to the millions of Americans who depend on us for their healthcare. Much of that value will be generated by AI and related concepts. WR offers learning modules in Human-Centered Design; Data Science; CMS Cloud; and AI and ML.

  • Pluralsight

    Pluralsight is an online educational platform that we use for our WR program. It allows us to combine pre-packaged educational materials with CMS-designed,CMS-specific content. Pluralsight is an important tool for creating a robust learning community at CMS where staff is empowered to acquire industry knowledge at the foundation of human-centered design, agile, and lean methodologies.

  • Data Science Track

    In the era of “Big Data”, it’s not hard to find information. Turning that information into knowledge, however, is another story. That’s what data science is all about. It’s an interdisciplinary field that uses scientific methods, processes, algorithms, and systems to extract insights from noisy, structured and unstructured data, and to apply those insights across a broad range of applications.

    Data science is reshaping healthcare in multiple applications, for example by improving diagnostic accuracy and optimizing clinical performance. CMS oversees multiple federal healthcare programs and generates an extensive amount of data daily that, when understood, can further streamline effective healthcare delivery to the American public. In sum, data science practices can help us reduce work burdens, make better decisions, and optimize solutions for our stakeholders. The practice of data science entails gathering data, examining it, sorting it, modeling it, interpreting it, and visualizing it. The Data Science Track of WR is your first step to empowering yourself with knowledge of these techniques.

    The Data Science Track will include three phases that will cater to different learning needs:

    • The Awareness phase: learning about Data Science
      • Scope of Data Science
      • Application of Data Science
      • The Life cycle of Data Science projects and common tools
      • CMS data and data literacy
    • The Competency phase: training your skills
      • Database and SQL
      • Data Manipulation and Analysis
      • Data Visualization and Storytelling
    • The Engagement phase: getting your hands dirty
      • Capstone projects as opportunities for real-life applications

    Delivery format: self-paced learning materials combined with instructor-led courses[FB1]

    Timeline: Starting FY2022
